Some quotes:

The world is mud-luscious and puddle-wonderful.

life's not a paragraph and death I think is no parenthesis

It takes courage to grow up and become who you really are.

         Today’s devotional speaks volume on the discernment of situations, implying an adjacent perspective, away from the obvious putative, substituting this for an inverse view, a clearly interesting proposition. Stoicism is about looking at things from every angle—and certain situations are easier to understand from different perspectives. In potentially negative situations, the objective, even superficial gaze might actually be superior. That view might let us see things clearly without diving too much into what they might represent or what might have caused them. In other situations, particularly those that involve something impressive or praiseworthy, another approach, like that of contemptuous expressions, is helpful. By examining situations from the inside out, we can be less daunted by them, less likely to be swayed by them.

         Dig into your fear of death or obscurity, and what will you find? Turn some fancy ceremony inside out and you’ll find—what?
